Legislation and Guidance

Understanding the legal framework for online safety

Key Legislation

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

Jurisdiction: United States

Purpose: Protects the privacy of children under 13 by requiring parental consent for the collection or use of any personal information.

Key Requirements:

  • Websites must obtain verifiable parental consent
  • Clear privacy policies must be provided
  • Reasonable security measures for children's data
  • Right to review and delete children's information

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Jurisdiction: European Union

Purpose: Gives individuals control over their personal data and simplifies regulations for international business.

Key Requirements:

  • Clear consent for data collection
  • Right to access and erase personal data
  • Data protection by design
  • Mandatory breach notifications

Online Safety Act

Jurisdiction: United Kingdom

Purpose: Makes the UK the safest place in the world to be online while protecting freedom of expression.

Key Requirements:

  • Duty of care on online platforms
  • Protection from illegal content
  • Protection for children from harmful content
  • Transparency and accountability measures

Best Practice Guidance

For Platforms

  • Implement robust age verification
  • Provide clear safety tools and reporting
  • Design with children's best interests
  • Transparent content moderation

For Schools

  • Comprehensive digital literacy programs
  • Clear social media policies
  • Staff training on online safety
  • Support systems for affected students

For Parents

  • Stay informed about platforms children use
  • Use parental controls appropriately
  • Model positive online behavior
  • Maintain open communication
