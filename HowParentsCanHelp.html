Supporting Your Teen's Online Safety

Practical strategies for parents and guardians

How Parents Can Help

As a parent, you play a crucial role in helping your teenager navigate social media safely. Here are evidence-based strategies to support your teen's digital wellbeing:

1. Open Communication

Establish regular, non-judgmental conversations about online experiences. Ask open-ended questions like:

  • "What do you enjoy most about the apps you use?"
  • "Have you ever seen anything online that made you uncomfortable?"
  • "How do you decide what to share and what to keep private?"

2. Set Clear Boundaries Together

Collaborate with your teen to establish reasonable guidelines:

  • Screen time limits that allow for homework and sleep
  • Device-free zones (e.g., dinner table, bedrooms)
  • Privacy settings that protect personal information

3. Educate About Digital Footprints

Help your teen understand that online content can be permanent. Discuss:

  • How colleges and employers review social media
  • The importance of thinking before posting
  • How to curate a positive online presence

Quick Safety Checks

Review these settings with your teen:

  • Privacy settings on all accounts
  • Location sharing permissions
  • Tagging and mention settings
  • Block and report functions

Recommended Parental Tools

These resources can help without being intrusive:

  • Family Link (Google) - Manage apps and screen time
  • Screen Time (Apple) - Set device limits
  • Bark - Monitors for concerning content
  • Common Sense Media - App reviews by age

When to Seek Help

Contact a professional if you notice:

  • Significant changes in mood or behavior
  • Withdrawal from offline activities
  • Extreme emotional responses to online interactions
  • Sleep disturbances related to device use
